The formula for F1 score is (Wikipedia): 

                    2 * true positive
F1 = ---------------------------------------------------
     2 * true positive + false negative + false positive

In Cluster Determinants, we introduce a small bias for larger files, by increasing the counts used in the formula by 1: 

                       2 * (true positive + 1)
F1 = ---------------------------------------------------------------------
     2 * (true positive + 1) + (false negative + 1) + (false positive + 1)

The effect is that items with very much data can have a maximum score close to 1, and a minimum close to 0, but that items with less data have a slightly lesser range.

The basis for the modification is based on the following. If we have two counts, A and B, then the proportion of A in the total is A/(A+B). But if A and B are based on small samples from a very large set, then the best prediction of the proportion of A in the larger set is (A+1)/(A+1+B+1), assuming a uniform distribution of chances for all possible proportions.
