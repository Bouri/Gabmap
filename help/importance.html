The measure of Importance is a recent, minor variation of the well known F1 score.

Importance introduces a correction for expected outcome on F1's Precision, and calls it Distinctiveness. But F1's Recall is renamed to Representativeness without such correction for expected outcome. This turns a symmetric measure into an asymmetric measure. (A further variation is to use the arithmetic mean instead of the harmonic mean, because you can't use the latter with negative fractions.)

Unlike the F1 score, Importance depends on the amount of false negatives in the data retrieval. This makes it more error prone in a situation where various amounts of data are unavailable. This is the reason why, in Gabmap, it can't be used in the fast method.

One author of Importance tells they wanted to introduce more weight to one part of the equation over the other part. Of course, the right way to do this is to use a known variant of the F1 score, for example the F2 score or the F0.5 score.

No tests are available that show that the measure of Importance has any advantage over the F1 score (or over any other known measure used in information retrieval).

Reference

Martijn Wieling & John Nerbonne. ‘Bipartite spectral graph partitioning for clustering dialect varieties and detecting their linguistic features’. Computer Speech and Language 25, 2011, pp 700-715. PDF
